About Kazakhstan

Explore the Heart of Eurasia

Kazakhstan is a vast country with diverse landscapes ranging from mountains to deserts. As the ninth largest country in the world, it is a melting pot of cultures, rich history, and breathtaking natural beauty. Known for its nomadic traditions, Kazakhstan offers a unique blend of modernity and heritage that captivates every traveler.

Geography

Kazakhstan is known for its diverse geography, featuring snow-capped mountains, vast steppes, and serene lakes. The Caspian Sea and Altai Mountains add to its natural splendor, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts and adventurers alike.

Languages Spoken

Kazakhstan has two official languages: Kazakh, the national language deeply rooted in the country's culture, and Russian, widely spoken for communication and business purposes.

Leisure Activities

From trekking in the Tian Shan Mountains to exploring the breathtaking Charyn Canyon, Kazakhstan offers countless outdoor adventures. Visitors can enjoy water sports on the Caspian Sea, explore ancient Silk Road routes, or dive into Almaty's bustling cultural scene.

Famous Delicacies of Kazakhstan

Experience the unique flavors of Kazakhstan with these traditional dishes that reflect the nation's rich culture and culinary heritage.

Beshbarmak

Known as the "five fingers" dish, Beshbarmak is a symbol of Kazakh hospitality. It consists of tender boiled meat, usually lamb or beef, served over thin, hand-rolled noodles. The dish is accompanied by a flavorful broth and often enjoyed communally with family and friends.

Kuurdak

A rustic and hearty dish, Kuurdak is made from pan-fried meat, onions, and potatoes. Often prepared with mutton or beef, it is one of the oldest traditional dishes in Kazakhstan and reflects the nomadic lifestyle of the Kazakh people.

Baursaks

These golden, fluffy fried dough balls are a staple of Kazakh cuisine. Served with tea, honey, or jam, Baursaks are enjoyed as both a savory and sweet treat. They are often prepared for special celebrations and family gatherings.

Shashlik

Shashlik, or grilled skewered meat, is a beloved delicacy enjoyed across Central Asia. Marinated in a blend of spices and vinegar, the meat is grilled over open flames, giving it a smoky and aromatic flavor. Served with raw onions and bread, it is a must-have at outdoor feasts.

Kazy

A delicacy reserved for special occasions, Kazy is a sausage made from horse meat and seasoned with salt, pepper, and garlic. It is often served as part of a traditional meat platter and is a favorite among Kazakhs for its rich flavor and cultural significance.

Kurt

Kurt is a traditional snack made from dried, salted cheese balls. With a tangy and salty taste, it is a popular travel food for Kazakh nomads. Packed with nutrients and long-lasting, Kurt is a symbol of the resourcefulness of Kazakh culinary traditions.

Top Places

Must-Visit Locations in Kazakhstan

Almaty

Almaty served as the capital of Kazakhstan until 1997 and is renowned for its role in the Silk Road trade, offering rich cultural and historical significance.

Astana

Astana, now Nur-Sultan, was established as the capital in 1997 and is known for its modern architecture reflecting Kazakhstan's development and heritage.

Charyn Canyon

Charyn Canyon has been shaped over millions of years and holds historical significance as part of ancient trade routes and nomadic settlements.

Khan Shatyr

Khan Shatyr, a modern architectural marvel, symbolizes Kazakhstan's progress and innovation in the heart of its capital.

Burabay National Park

Burabay National Park is steeped in legends and history, once serving as a retreat for ancient Kazakh tribes and leaders.
